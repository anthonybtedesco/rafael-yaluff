RAFAEL YALUFF

THE HIDDEN KING

I watch the storm eat the sky like The Nothingness swallowing Fantasia, and I paint that feeling. Somewhere between real and not real, between a shape and a sigh.

I chew on something and nothing, drag it across the canvas. Love and hunger, paint and silence. It's all there, buzzing under the noise.

I keep thinking, paintings should be simple. Like a Nirvana song: three chords, a storm in your chest, hits you fast, won't tell you why.

I've eaten a hundred styles, spit out a thousand lies. Rebuilt my own art history, maybe that's just the Latino in me, digging through the rubble for something soft, a new self hiding behind the next beautiful break.
